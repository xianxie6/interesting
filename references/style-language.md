# Interesting visual language

## Core formula

`image = emotional field + one or two extracted cores + zero or one action device + zero or one relationship structure + three to five rhythmic repetitions`

The distinctive feature is not merely thick black outlines. It is the interaction of:

- hard geometry as conceptual nouns;
- simplified source silhouettes and spatial relationships;
- dots and connectors as systems or relationships.

Hands and other human gestures are optional, not mandatory. For source-image work, preserve the actual content before applying any standard composition or metaphor.

Treat a source collection, triptych, postcard, or framed scene as one semantic core when its identity depends on several peer objects. Preserve the necessary members instead of forcing the source into an arbitrary two-object limit.

## Color system

Use no more than three colors per image:

- line: near-black `#141413`;
- primary fill: warm white `#FAF9F6`;
- background: choose one flat color below.

| Background | Hex | Semantic use |
|---|---|---|
| Fog blue | `#7497B9` | technology, knowledge, global, clarity, reason |
| Mint gray | `#C0D1CB` | collaboration, adjustment, calm, interface, process |
| Moss green | `#7D8C63` | growth, ecology, accumulation, stability, long term |
| Mist lilac | `#CBCAD9` | thinking, protection, abstraction, perception, introspection |
| Terracotta | `#CD7C5E` | action, creation, communication, energy, change |
| Paper cream | `#F0EEE7` | classification, archive, atlas, culture, system |
| Dusty blush | `#E6D0CF` | relationship, care, transition, gentleness |
| Muted rose | `#B86C86` | focus, emotion, health, alert, force |
| Sand beige | `#E2DACD` | reading, time, balance, craft |

Use solid fills only. Forbid gradients, transparency effects, highlights, shadows, and material rendering. Keep black as lines and compact nodes, not a large background field.

## Two-colorway pairing

Return two versions of the same design by default. Change only the flat background color; preserve linework and warm-white fills exactly.

Recommended pairs:

- terracotta `#CD7C5E` + moss green `#7D8C63`: energetic versus grounded;
- fog blue `#7497B9` + dusty blush `#E6D0CF`: rational versus gentle;
- mint gray `#C0D1CB` + mist lilac `#CBCAD9`: calm process versus introspection;
- muted rose `#B86C86` + paper cream `#F0EEE7`: strong focus versus quiet editorial.

Select pairs from the concept's emotional range, not randomly. Ensure the two backgrounds differ enough to be judged immediately. Label them A and B with names and hex values outside the image; keep the image itself text-free.

## Line behavior

- Width: about 1.4%–2.2% of the canvas short edge.
- Caps and joins: round.
- Pressure: nearly consistent with slight natural variation.
- Wobble: controlled and visible, never perfect Bézier geometry.
- Closures: may be slightly imperfect.
- Curves: feel drawn in one continuous motion.
- Anatomy: omit fingernails, knuckles, texture, and realistic perspective.
- Tone: intelligent and human, neither polished corporate iconography nor childish doodling.

## Shape vocabulary

### Conceptual nouns

- circle or irregular polygon: whole, world, object, focus, core;
- square or paper: information, task, module, page;
- triangle: direction, choice, support, warning, upward motion;
- steps or bars: growth, progress, upgrade, stages;
- house: belonging, institution, organization, protection;
- book, paper, or screen: knowledge, expression, presentation, digital work;
- hourglass or balance: time, tradeoff, balance, transformation;
- keyhole or lock: permission, security, privacy, access.

### Relationships

- dots and lines: connection, system, network, data, distribution;
- radial structure: expansion, influence, generation, possibility;
- grid or globe: global, infrastructure, shared space;
- bracket or frame: code, scope, rule, container, context;
- arrow or polyline: direction, transformation, feedback, growth path;
- repeated circles or squares: group, scale, modularity;
- direct link: bridge, cooperation, causality, migration.

### Action devices

Use the least invented device that makes the source action or conceptual verb readable:

- existing pose or direction: retain the source's visible action;
- relative position: support, hierarchy, distance, tension, belonging;
- displacement or deformation: change, pressure, growth, simplification;
- connector or motion path: link, sequence, transfer, feedback;
- repeated state: scale, accumulation, rhythm, progression;
- tool or hand: use only when visible in the source, explicitly requested, or necessary to express the concept.

When a hand is justified, draw it as a continuous black outline with three or four looped fingers. Exaggerate it slightly and allow entry from outside the frame. Do not draw realistic palms, fingernails, or knuckles.

## Composition and rhythm

- Main subject occupies 55%–75% of the canvas.
- Keep 8%–15% outer breathing room.
- Preserve the source's dominant left/right, top/bottom, scale, direction, and crop before introducing asymmetry.
- Let a justified hand or off-canvas object enter from an edge only when the source or concept requires it.
- Keep 60%–75% of the image as quiet background or negative space.
- Carry only one primary judgment per image.
- Use 2×2 or 3×3 grids only for atlases, capability sets, or taxonomies.

Build rhythm with three to five repetitions:

- three or four looped fingers;
- three steps or bars;
- three to five nodes or branches;
- three parallel lines or arcs;
- three or four adjacent geometric blocks.

Rhythm formula: `one large mass + one medium relationship + three to five small repeats`.

## Standard compositions

For image inputs, first use **Source-preserving**. Use the other templates only when they match the source's actual arrangement or when generating from text.

1. **Source-preserving**: retain the source's dominant silhouette, direction, relative positions, crop, and repeated motif; simplify internal detail without changing the scene's identity.
2. **Support**: a core sits above a base, ground, pedestal, branch, or justified hand. Use for support, knowledge, home, value.
3. **Operate**: paper, screen, slider, or module is being changed by an existing control, tool, or justified hand. Use for control, selection, editing, product capability.
4. **Co-create**: geometry overlaps or connects from multiple directions; hands are optional. Use for collaboration, community, shared creation.
5. **Transform**: two states connect by displacement, deformation, line, steps, arrow, or handoff. Use for growth, migration, simplification, change.
6. **System**: a network sits on a warm-white circle, paper, grid, or repeated module. Use for AI, data, global networks, infrastructure.
7. **Container**: brackets, house, book, screen, or paper encloses the core. Use for rules, safety, knowledge, code, organization.
8. **Atlas**: 2×2 or 3×3 symbol grid with at most one accent-colored unit. Use for toolkits, taxonomies, visual indexes.
9. **Postcard scene**: a frame encloses one anchor subject and several repeated environmental motifs. Use only when the source's identity depends on a dense scene, stamp, postcard, or editorial vignette.

## Semantic compression

Map:

- noun → one archetypal object or geometric container;
- action → an existing pose, relative position, displacement, deformation, connector, motion path, tool, or justified hand;
- relationship → a connector, position, repeated module, or enclosure;
- emotion → one fixed background color.

Then reduce:

- at most two semantic cores; a necessary collection or framed scene may count as one core;
- at most one relationship structure;
- zero hands by default;
- use one hand only when the source, user, or concept clearly justifies it;
- use two or three hands only when the actual subject is collaboration, exchange, or collective shaping;
- no text unless explicitly requested;
- no decorative symbols that do not alter the message.

For a source image, test the result beside the source: a viewer should recognize the same dominant subject and spatial relationship within two seconds. For text input, hide the title and test whether the core relationship remains inferable.
